Saint Maud

Rating: 9.5/10

Director: Rosie Glass

Main Cast: Jennifer Ehle, Lily Frazer, Lily Knight, Marcus Hutton, Turlough Convery and Rosie Sansom

Release Date: July 1, 2021

Genre: Psychological Horror


Review

The Saint Maud feels like watching someone fall apart but what makes it so haunting and disturbing is how deeply how human Maud is, like she's awkward, lonely and trying hard to be good, and in the movie, you can feel how badly she wants her life to matter. Instead of the typical religious horror villain the movie shows her vulnereability first so as when things start breaking down, she too breaks down and finally loses it. And in her final moments feels like a punch to the chest. The movie lets you see the world through her eyes for just a glimpse, to make you understand how desperately she needs something bigger than herself. Then reality washed through, it was harsh, cruel and suffocating. Saint Maud leaves you wondering about the people who are suffering the same thing as Maud, who fall to the breaking point of their lives, who suffers quietly and how easily belief can becone a lifeline. It's tragic knowing the feeling that Maud didn't need saving from any demons, she just needed saving from her own loneliness. Close

Hereditary

Rating: 8.9/10

Director: Ari Aster

Main Cast: Toni Collette, Alex Wolff, Milly Shapiro, Ann Dowd, and Gabriel Byrne

Release Date: January 21, 2018

Genre: Psychological horror


Review

Hereditary works so well because it mixes real family tension with slow, creeping horror. You can feel the stress and sadness in the house, and the characters react in ways that feel very human and believable. It's uncomfortable, but in a way that makes you understand their struggle. By the end, the movie becomes more intense and shocking, but it still feels grounded in the family's pain and confusion. It's disturbing, emotional, and memorable without being over-explained — a horror story that sticks with you because of the people at its center. Close

Midsommar

Rating: 8/10

Director: Ari Aster

Main Cast: Florence Pugh, Jack Reynor William Jackson Harper, Vilhelm Blomgren, Ellora Torchia, Archie Madekwe, and Will Poulter.

Release Date: June 18, 2019

Genre: Psychological horror


Review

Midsommar feels like watching someone's heartbreak slowly bloom into something terrifying and strangely freeing. The movie wraps grief, loneliness, and emotional neglect inside this bright, peaceful setting that makes everything feel even more unsettling. You don't just see Dani's pain — you feel the way she clings to anything that makes her feel understood. By the end, the film becomes less about horror and more about a woman finally letting go of everything that's been crushing her. It's tragic, disturbing, and oddly cathartic all at once. It leaves you thinking not just about the cult, but about how deeply people need to feel seen and cared for — even when they find it in the wrong place.

Martys

Rating: 9/10

Director: Pascal Laugier

Main Cast: Morjana Alaoui, Mylène Jampanoï, and Catherine Bégin

Release Date: September 3, 2008

Genre: Psychological horror film


Review

I honestly love this movie! it shows you how far can people go just to seek answers, find the truth. Martys isn't your typical Gore horror movie, beneath of all those brutality and torture, theres a deep sadness running throughout the whole story. The first few minutes feel shocking and tragic watching the young woman's life. As you continue to watch, you can feel her desperation, the way her pain never left her as she escaped the slaughterhouse. The second half shifts into something even heavier and tragic. A story about faith, endurance and how far humans can go just to gain something. In the end, neither of them wins, it just leaves you quiet, hollow and shocked. After watching the movie you'd be thinking about what it means to push someone past the limits of their humanity. This movie is devastating and heartbreaking as for the protagonist, antogonist and the viewers.
